Who I Am

I'm Bernard Ndeogo Issifu — a GISP-certified geospatial data scientist embedded at the Ohio Department of Transportation through CDM Smith, applying machine learning and spatial analytics to real transportation and infrastructure problems.

My daily stack is Python, ArcGIS Pro, and SQL. I build analytical workflows, spatial models, and data-driven deliverables — from EV charging gap analyses using OpenStreetMap API to microsimulation models for major Ohio corridor studies.

Currently completing an MS in Data Science at the University of Arizona, building toward production-grade geospatial data science: cloud infrastructure on GCP, ML pipelines with Apache Airflow, PostGIS spatial databases, and computer vision for remote sensing.

My capstone builds a real-time wildfire detection pipeline ingesting NASA FIRMS thermal data, Sentinel-2 imagery, and NOAA feeds into a PostGIS database — with a CNN smoke classifier deployed on Google Cloud Platform.

03 — Projects

Work & Research

In Progress
Real-Time Wildfire Detection Pipeline
An end-to-end geospatial ML pipeline for near-real-time wildfire monitoring. NASA FIRMS thermal anomaly data (updated every 10 minutes), Sentinel-2 multispectral imagery, NOAA weather feeds, and USGS elevation data are ingested into a PostGIS spatial database. Apache Airflow orchestrates all pipeline jobs. A CNN smoke classifier automatically scores incoming imagery and flags high-probability fire events. Deployed on Google Cloud Platform with a live Kepler.gl spatial dashboard. MS Data Science Capstone — University of Arizona.
