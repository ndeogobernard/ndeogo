Flood Potential Assessment ‚Äî Santa Cruz River Basin
A spatial ML model predicting flood probability at the parcel level for the Santa Cruz River Basin in Arizona. High-resolution LiDAR DEMs provide elevation, slope, and flow accumulation features. Historical FEMA flood event data and NOAA Atlas 14 precipitation records serve as training labels. A random forest classifier is trained on these spatial features and validated with a confusion matrix and cross-validation. Results are visualized as a flood risk index map overlaid on Census vulnerability data to identify the most exposed and least resourced communities.

EV Charging Site Suitability ‚Äî Columbus, OH
A multi-criteria spatial optimization model identifying the best locations for heavy-duty EV charging hubs in Columbus, OH. Criteria ‚Äî highway proximity, grid capacity, land use zoning, population density, and existing charger gaps ‚Äî are weighted using an Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) implemented in Python rather than manually in ArcGIS, making the methodology reproducible and defensible. A sensitivity analysis tests how results shift under different weight configurations. Outputs include a weighted overlay suitability map and an interactive ArcGIS Online web map for stakeholder review.

Land Use Change Detection ‚Äî Central Ohio Urban Sprawl
A 20-year remote sensing study of urban expansion in Central Ohio using Landsat 8/9 multispectral imagery. Rather than relying on pre-classified NLCD data, raw satellite bands are classified using a supervised SVM classifier trained on labeled ground truth points ‚Äî validated with a confusion matrix. A change detection map shows land cover transitions (agricultural ‚Üí urban, forest ‚Üí suburban) and a time-series chart tracks the proportion of impervious surface growth by year. A changepoint detection algorithm identifies when conversion rates accelerated most significantly.

Historical Hurricane Impact Analysis
A spatiotemporal analysis of hurricane impacts on Gulf and Atlantic Coast infrastructure and communities using NOAA HURDAT2 storm track data (1980‚Äì2023). Storm characteristics ‚Äî wind speed, storm surge extent, rainfall, track proximity ‚Äî are joined with FEMA individual assistance records and Census socioeconomic data at the county level. An animated storm track map and damage choropleth are produced as key deliverables.
